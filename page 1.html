*What are the symptoms of corona virus?

Signs and symptoms of COVID-19 may appear two to 14 days after exposure. The period following exposure and prior to the onset of symptoms is called the incubation period. Common signs and symptoms can include: 1- fever, 2-cough, 3-Tired, 4-Early symptoms of COVID-19 may include loss of taste or smell. #Other symptoms can include: 1-Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing, 2-muscle pain, 3-chills, 4-Sore throat, 5-Runny nose, 6-headache, 7-chest pain, 8-pink eye (conjunctivitis), 9-nausea, 10-vomiting, 11-diarrhea, 12-Skin rash, This list is not exhaustive. Children usually have symptoms similar to adults, and their illness is generally mild. The severity of COVID-19 symptoms can range from very mild to severe. Some people may have only a few symptoms, and some people may have no symptoms at all. Some people may experience worsening of symptoms, such as worsening shortness of breath and worsening pneumonia, about a week after symptoms be Older people are at greater risk of developing severe COVID-19 symptoms, and that risk increases as a person gets older. People with pre-existing medical conditions may be more likely to develop serious symptoms. Examples of serious health conditions that increase your risk of developing severe COVID-19 symptoms include: Serious heart disease, such as heart failure, coronary artery disease, or cardiomyopathy 1-cancer, 2-Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), 3-type 2 diabetes, 4-Obesity or severe obesity, 5-smoking, 6-Chronic kidney disease, 7-sickle cell disease, 8-Weakened immune system due to solid organ transplants, 9-pregnancy, #There are other conditions that increase the risk of serious symptoms, such as: 1-asthma, 2-Liver disease, 3-overweight, 4-Chronic lung diseases, such as cystic fibrosis or pulmonary fibrosis, 5-Medical conditions related to the brain and nervous system, 6-Weakened immune system due to bone marrow transplant, HIV, or certain medications, 7-Type 1 diabetes, 8-Hypertension
